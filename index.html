Project 02

Energy Optimization

λ: LAGRANGIAN STATIONARY ∇f: EFFICIENCY GRADIENT MULTIVARIATE CALCULUS

HYDRO-TURBINE
EFFICIENCY

Formulated a constrained optimization model to maximize total power output across three hydroelectric turbines, each governed by a distinct quadratic function of water flow rate derived from Bernoulli's equation and experimental data. Applied the method of Lagrange multipliers — solving the stationary points of L(x, y, λ) = f(x, y) − λ(g(x, y) − c) — to determine the optimal flow distribution among turbines subject to individual capacity constraints. Derived closed-form expressions for each turbine's flow as a linear function of total flow Q_T, enabling rapid computation of the optimal split at any inflow level. Simulated efficiency gradients and power curves in MATLAB, benchmarked the optimized allocation against equal distribution (which yielded ~158 kW less power at Q_T = 2500 ft³/s), and extended the analysis to two-turbine configurations. Results demonstrated that Turbines 1 and 3 form the dominant pair for mid-range flows, and identified the upper feasibility bound beyond which no valid optimization exists.
